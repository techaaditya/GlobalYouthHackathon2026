// FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers for nominees, prospective sponsors, and general observers about the Forum's programme and logistics.

The Global Youth Forum is a high-profile assembly bringing together 200 selected youth delegates, tech researchers, and student ambassadors at the UNESCO Headquarters in China. Organized in partnership with the Bagmati UNESCO Club, the forum serves as a bridge, fusing practical AI development in technical sandboxes with strict multilateral policy guidelines to draft the Youth AI Ethics Accord.
The Sandbox Track is tailored for programmers, engineers, and digital ethicists to build, test, and audit algorithmic watermarks for generative AI models. The Policy Track is designed for young diplomats, researchers, and legal scholars to negotiate treaty frameworks, standard-setting guidelines, and structural recommendations based on UNESCO's 2021 global standards.
Delegates go through a multi-stage vetting process. First, passport credentials and academic portfolios must match server-side standards. Nominees then complete remote sandbox sprints, peer feedback sessions, or Riyadh onboarding programs before receiving their official delegation accreditation credentials.
Yes! Selected nominees who demonstrate strong contributions in their local pre-summit hackathons may apply for accommodation and travel grants. These grants are coordinated directly with the Secretariat and the Bagmati UNESCO Club delegation offices to cover physical attendance costs in China.
The Youth AI Ethics Accord is the final, binding master declaration co-signed by all 200 accredited delegates. It aggregates recommendations on algorithmic biases, user transparency, data sovereignty, and human-in-the-loop safeguards to be formally presented to state secretaries and global leaders.
Academic institutions, non-profits, and sovereign delegations can request formal partnership or co-convenor status. Sponsors can secure naming rights for sandbox theme rooms or fund dedicated travel grants for underrepresented digital researchers.
